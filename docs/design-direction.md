# Deebaj Royal Design Direction

## Primary visual reference

Use the current Deebaj Royal website identity as the main source of truth:

- warm ivory background
- brown honey brand surfaces
- restrained luxury gold accents
- Arabic-first luxury typography
- spaced Deebaj Royal logo styling
- calm premium tone
- soft, elegant spacing

## Competitor UX reference notes

These references are for UX learning only. Do not copy their layouts, colors, or
visual language.

### Qoufi / Govyy

What works:

- strong offer and bundle framing
- visible FAQ/trust content
- many customer reviews
- direct product benefit language

Risks to avoid:

- crowded promotional rhythm
- repeated proof points
- product value can feel price-led instead of premium-led

### Ghaim

What works:

- product-page storytelling around comfort and usage
- clear feature bullets
- gift/premium positioning

Risks to avoid:

- long product copy without enough hierarchy
- limited brand-level navigation context

### Rain / Rhine

What works:

- direct product categories
- clear pack-size/value descriptions
- straightforward shipping and payment reassurance

Risks to avoid:

- generic ecommerce presentation
- discount-led hierarchy
- limited emotional storytelling

### Athlah

What works:

- clear category structure
- FAQ and payment/shipping trust answers
- simple customer review presence

Risks to avoid:

- minimal visual differentiation
- functional tone can feel less premium
- broad categories can dilute luxury focus

## Deebaj Royal target

Deebaj Royal should feel more premium and easier to use than these references by
combining:

- Apple-level spacing and restraint
- Aesop-level minimalism
- Arabic-first luxury typography
- hospitality-grade trust and service clarity
- concise product storytelling
- clean mobile-first product and checkout paths

Phase 1 should remain a minimal route/component foundation. Full checkout,
payments, advanced animation, and production integrations belong to later
phases.
